# Advanced Model Notes: Food Chemistry and the Molecular Basis of Nutrition

This advanced layer is designed to make the GitHub repository more credible as a computational science companion to the article.

## What this layer adds

- nutrient-density proxy
- bioavailability-adjusted iron
- retained vitamin C estimate
- digestible protein estimate
- protein-quality proxy
- food-matrix protection index
- glycemic accessibility proxy
- lipid oxidation vulnerability
- sodium pressure index
- chemical safety attention index
- nutrition chemistry quality index
- processing-retention scenarios
- lipid oxidation scenarios
- glycemic matrix scenarios
- mineral bioavailability scenarios
- SQL-ready provenance structure
- lightweight tests

## Important simplifications

The workflow is intentionally educational. It does not include:

- clinical nutrition assessment
- dietary recommendation generation
- individualized nutrient requirements
- allergen clearance
- regulatory nutrient-content claims
- health-claim substantiation
- actual laboratory food analysis
- microbial food-safety modeling
- validated glycemic-index prediction
- validated protein-quality scoring
- full amino-acid scoring
- full fatty-acid oxidation chemistry
- shelf-life validation
- sensory panel data
- contaminant exposure assessment
- food-label compliance review
- population dietary modeling

## Interpretation

The output indices are screening and teaching indicators. They help explain how food chemistry connects molecular composition, food structure, processing, bioavailability, safety, and nutrition, but they are not dietary advice, clinical findings, regulatory determinations, food-safety findings, allergen determinations, or product claims.

## Responsible-use boundary

Do not use these outputs for patient care, diet planning, clinical nutrition, infant feeding, pregnancy nutrition, allergy management, food-safety certification, regulatory labeling, public-health guidance, product claims, or purchasing decisions without qualified professional review.
