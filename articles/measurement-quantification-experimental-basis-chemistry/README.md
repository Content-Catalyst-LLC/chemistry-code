# Measurement, Quantification, and the Experimental Basis of Chemistry

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Measurement, Quantification, and the Experimental Basis of Chemistry

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for chemical measurement concepts, including mass-volume-concentration calculations, dilution planning, calibration curves, replicate precision, uncertainty, traceability metadata, SQL schemas, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Mass, volume, amount of substance, and molarity.
2. Dilution planning using C1V1 = C2V2.
3. Linear calibration and unknown concentration estimation.
4. Replicate measurements, mean, standard deviation, and relative standard deviation.
5. Expanded uncertainty as U = kuc.
6. SQL-backed measurement metadata.
7. Provenance and reproducibility documentation.
8. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not laboratory protocols, chemical safety systems, regulatory tools, analytical validation systems, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
