# Electronic and Photochemical Materials Reproducibility Checklist

- Record material composition, phase, morphology, processing route, and atmosphere.
- Preserve absorption, emission, photoluminescence, and electrical measurement data.
- Report device architecture, contacts, active area, illumination conditions, and calibration.
- Track scan direction, hysteresis, stabilization time, and measurement history.
- Include replicate measurements and uncertainty.
- Test photostability, thermal stability, humidity stability, oxygen sensitivity, and bias stress where relevant.
- Characterize interfaces, defects, surface states, and degradation pathways.
- Document critical materials, toxicity, recovery, and end-of-life assumptions.
- Distinguish initial performance from long-term operating evidence.
