# Responsible Use Notes

Semiconductor, electronic, and photochemical materials can influence energy systems, sensors, photochemical synthesis, electronics, photovoltaics, displays, and environmental technologies. The examples in this folder are not validated for those purposes.

## Do not use this folder for

- device certification;
- photovoltaic performance claims;
- photochemical safety decisions;
- procurement or investment decisions;
- industrial process design;
- environmental performance claims;
- regulatory reporting;
- product qualification.

## Recommended real-world extensions

- validate band gaps using appropriate optical and electronic methods;
- report device area, illumination, calibration, scan direction, and hysteresis controls;
- test stability under light, heat, oxygen, moisture, bias, and mechanical stress;
- preserve raw spectroscopy, device, and degradation data;
- characterize defects, grain boundaries, surfaces, and interfaces;
- document solvents, processing temperature, dopants, contacts, encapsulation, and device architecture;
- evaluate critical materials, toxicity, recovery, recycling, and end-of-life pathways;
- distinguish screening results from validated application readiness.
