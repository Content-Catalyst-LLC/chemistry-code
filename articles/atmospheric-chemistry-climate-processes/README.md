# Atmospheric Chemistry and Climate Processes

This article folder supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

**Atmospheric Chemistry and Climate Processes**

The repository materials provide reproducible, educational workflows for atmospheric composition screening, greenhouse-gas forcing approximations, ozone benchmark screening, first-order atmospheric lifetime modeling, simplified photochemical diagnostics, aerosol indicators, and provenance-aware atmospheric monitoring data structures.

## Contents

- `data/` — synthetic atmospheric monitoring and emissions-context data
- `python/` — greenhouse forcing, ozone screening, and first-order lifetime workflow
- `r/` — atmospheric monitoring summary workflow
- `julia/` — simplified photochemical ozone and forcing calculations
- `fortran/` — carbon dioxide radiative forcing approximation
- `rust/` — atmospheric screening CLI example
- `go/` — greenhouse-gas and ozone screening example
- `c/` — atmospheric lifetime and first-order decay example
- `cpp/` — simplified methane oxidation lifetime example
- `sql/` — atmospheric monitoring schema and sample queries
- `docs/` — assumptions, QA/QC notes, and responsible-use guidance
- `notebooks/` — reproducible notebook scaffold
- `outputs/` — generated tables, reports, figures, and provenance manifests

## Scientific scope

These examples are simplified and educational. They are intended to show how atmospheric chemistry connects:

- atmospheric concentration and mixing ratio
- greenhouse-gas forcing approximations
- photochemical ozone screening
- aerosol and particulate indicators
- atmospheric lifetime and first-order loss
- methane, carbon monoxide, and oxidizing capacity
- quality-assured atmospheric monitoring
- source-pathway-receptor interpretation
- climate-chemistry data provenance

They are not regulatory tools and should not be used for air-quality compliance, climate attribution, emergency response, health advisories, or legal determinations without qualified expert review.
