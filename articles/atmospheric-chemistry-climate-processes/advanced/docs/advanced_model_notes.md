# Advanced Model Notes: Atmospheric Chemistry and Climate Processes

This advanced layer is designed to make the GitHub repository more credible as a computational science companion to the article.

## What this layer adds

- reference-ratio screening
- simplified greenhouse forcing proxies
- atmospheric lifetime persistence
- photochemical ozone formation proxy
- aerosol direct-effect proxy
- aerosol pressure index
- oxidizing-capacity stress index
- composite atmospheric chemistry pressure index
- QA-weighted evidence score
- greenhouse forcing scenario series
- methane lifetime decay scenario
- ozone photochemical sensitivity grid
- aerosol optical-depth sensitivity grid
- SQL-ready provenance structure
- lightweight tests

## Important simplifications

The workflow is intentionally educational. It does not include:

- full IPCC radiative-forcing formulae with overlap terms
- chemical transport modeling
- emissions inventories
- OH radical chemistry mechanisms
- VOC speciation
- NOx-limited versus VOC-limited ozone regimes
- heterogeneous aerosol chemistry
- aerosol-cloud interactions
- stratospheric chemistry
- uncertainty propagation
- meteorological transport
- regulatory compliance rules

## Interpretation

The output indices are screening and teaching indicators. They help explain how atmospheric chemistry connects molecular measurements, photochemistry, aerosols, greenhouse gases, and climate processes, but they are not scientific findings, compliance determinations, health advisories, or forecasts.

## Responsible-use boundary

Do not use these outputs for air-quality compliance, public-health decisions, climate attribution, emissions policy, legal evidence, operational forecasting, wildfire smoke guidance, or regulatory reporting.
