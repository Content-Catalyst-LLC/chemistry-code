# Model Assumptions

The computational examples in this article folder are simplified teaching tools.

## CO2 radiative forcing

The approximate expression

Delta F = 5.35 ln(C / C0)

is used to show the relationship between CO2 concentration and radiative forcing. It is not a full climate model and does not include feedbacks, ocean uptake, clouds, aerosols, land-surface processes, or regional climate response.

## First-order atmospheric lifetime

The first-order decay examples assume:

- no continuing emissions
- a well-mixed box
- constant first-order loss
- no transport
- no chemical regime shift
- no deposition variation
- no diurnal photochemistry

This is useful for illustrating lifetime and half-life but should not be treated as a complete atmospheric fate model.

## Ozone production index

The ozone-production index is a pedagogical proxy:

index = sunlight * sqrt(NOx * VOC)

It is not a chemical mechanism. Real ozone formation depends on nonlinear photochemistry, NOx/VOC regime, meteorology, radical recycling, deposition, transport, background ozone, clouds, aerosols, and emissions timing.

## Methane oxidation

The methane oxidation lifetime example treats hydroxyl radical concentration as constant. Real methane lifetime is controlled by global OH fields, stratospheric loss, soil uptake, atmospheric transport, and coupled emissions of CO, NOx, VOCs, and methane itself.
