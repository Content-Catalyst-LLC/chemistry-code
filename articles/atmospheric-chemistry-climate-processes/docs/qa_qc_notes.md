# QA/QC Notes for Atmospheric Chemistry Data

Atmospheric chemistry data require careful metadata and quality assurance.

## Recommended metadata

- station identifier
- latitude, longitude, and altitude
- sampling height
- sampling date and time
- time zone or UTC convention
- averaging period
- analyte
- unit
- measurement method
- calibration scale
- detection limit
- uncertainty estimate
- inlet information
- meteorological conditions
- quality-control flag
- instrument maintenance records
- data-processing version
- reference or benchmark context

## Common quality issues

- mixing incompatible averaging periods
- comparing volume mixing ratios and mass concentrations without conversion
- missing humidity and temperature context
- instrument drift
- inlet losses
- calibration-scale mismatch
- boundary-layer effects
- representativeness errors
- satellite retrieval biases
- cloud contamination in remote sensing
- failure to distinguish background and source-influenced observations

## Practical principle

Atmospheric chemistry observations are most useful when they are traceable, calibrated, time-resolved, geolocated, and accompanied by uncertainty and quality flags.
