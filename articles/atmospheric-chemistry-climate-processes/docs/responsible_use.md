# Responsible Use Notes

These files are educational examples for chemistry communication, computational learning, and reproducible workflow design.

They should not be used as:

- regulatory air-quality compliance tools
- emergency-response guidance
- public-health advisories
- climate attribution studies
- emissions verification systems
- legal evidence
- occupational exposure determinations
- environmental justice screening determinations
- operational air-quality forecasts

Atmospheric chemistry decisions can affect health, infrastructure, ecosystems, public trust, and climate policy. Real applications require validated instruments, qualified atmospheric scientists, appropriate standards, uncertainty analysis, emissions context, meteorology, and transparent communication with affected communities.

The examples intentionally use synthetic data.
