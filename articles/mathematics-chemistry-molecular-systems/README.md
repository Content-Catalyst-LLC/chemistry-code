# Mathematics for Chemistry and Molecular Systems

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Mathematics for Chemistry and Molecular Systems

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for mathematical chemistry concepts, including stoichiometry, units, concentration, pH, kinetics, thermodynamics, equilibrium, molecular geometry, linear algebra, uncertainty, graph representations, SQL metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Stoichiometric ratios and conservation.
2. pH and logarithmic scale.
3. First-order kinetic decay.
4. Thermodynamic relationship between standard free energy and equilibrium.
5. Molecular geometry from coordinates.
6. Linear algebra and eigenvalue examples.
7. Replicate statistics and uncertainty.
8. Graph-style molecular connectivity.
9. SQL-backed mathematical chemistry metadata.
10. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not laboratory protocols, chemical safety systems, regulatory tools, analytical validation systems, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
