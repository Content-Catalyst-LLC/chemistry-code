# Model Assumptions

The computational examples in this folder are simplified teaching tools.

## Soil organic carbon stock

The example uses:

Mg C/ha = SOC_percent * bulk_density_g_cm3 * depth_cm

This compact expression is useful for teaching, but real soil carbon accounting requires careful depth harmonization, rock-fragment correction, equivalent soil mass, bulk-density uncertainty, method compatibility, and repeated measurements over time.

## Base saturation

The base saturation calculation assumes base cations and CEC are expressed in compatible charge-equivalent units. Real interpretation depends on method, pH, clay mineralogy, organic matter, salinity, sodicity, and crop or ecosystem context.

## Nutrient screening

The phosphorus and nitrate flags are illustrative. They are not fertilizer recommendations, environmental thresholds, regulatory standards, or watershed-loss predictions.

## Nitrogen balance

The nitrogen balance example is a simple mass-balance illustration. Real nitrogen cycling requires crop uptake timing, mineralization, immobilization, nitrification, denitrification, volatilization, leaching, runoff, weather, drainage, soil texture, and management data.
