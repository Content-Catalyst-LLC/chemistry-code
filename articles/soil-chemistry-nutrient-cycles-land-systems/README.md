# Soil Chemistry, Nutrient Cycles, and Land Systems

This article folder supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

**Soil Chemistry, Nutrient Cycles, and Land Systems**

The repository materials provide reproducible, educational workflows for soil fertility screening, soil organic carbon stock estimation, nutrient balance, cation exchange interpretation, base saturation, pH flags, phosphorus runoff attention, nitrate leaching attention, and provenance-aware soil monitoring data structures.

## Contents

- `data/` — synthetic soil monitoring and land-system data
- `python/` — soil fertility screening, SOC stocks, base saturation, and nutrient flags
- `r/` — soil monitoring summaries and nitrogen balance
- `julia/` — soil organic carbon stock and nutrient balance calculations
- `fortran/` — soil organic carbon stock calculation
- `rust/` — command-line soil screening example
- `go/` — nutrient balance and base saturation example
- `c/` — soil pH and salinity screening example
- `cpp/` — cation exchange capacity and base saturation example
- `sql/` — soil monitoring schema and sample queries
- `docs/` — QA/QC notes, model assumptions, and responsible-use guidance
- `notebooks/` — reproducible notebook scaffold
- `outputs/` — generated tables, reports, figures, and provenance manifests

## Scientific scope

These examples are simplified and educational. They are designed to show how soil chemistry connects:

- pH and acidity
- cation exchange capacity
- base saturation
- soil organic carbon stocks
- nitrogen and phosphorus cycling
- nutrient management and environmental loss
- land use and downstream water quality
- soil monitoring metadata
- contamination and responsible interpretation

They are not agronomic recommendations, regulatory determinations, contamination assessments, soil-carbon verification tools, legal evidence, or public-health advisories.
