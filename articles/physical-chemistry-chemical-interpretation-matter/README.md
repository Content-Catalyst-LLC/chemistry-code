# Physical Chemistry and the Chemical Interpretation of Matter

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Physical Chemistry and the Chemical Interpretation of Matter

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for physical chemistry, including thermodynamic state functions, equilibrium constants, Arrhenius kinetics, Boltzmann populations, diffusion, electrochemical free energy, Nernst relationships, SQL-backed physical chemistry metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Gibbs free energy and equilibrium constants.
2. Reaction quotient free-energy relationships.
3. Arrhenius temperature dependence.
4. Boltzmann populations.
5. Diffusion finite-difference scaffolds.
6. Electrochemical free energy.
7. Nernst equation scaffolds.
8. Physical constants and units.
9. SQL-backed physical chemistry metadata.
10. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not validated laboratory systems, process controls, safety models, regulatory systems, clinical tools, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
