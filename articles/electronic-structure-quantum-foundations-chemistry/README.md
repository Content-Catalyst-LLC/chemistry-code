# Electronic Structure and the Quantum Foundations of Chemistry

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Electronic Structure and the Quantum Foundations of Chemistry

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for electronic structure, hydrogen energy levels, photon transitions, orbital capacities, electron configurations, effective nuclear charge, particle-in-a-box models, small Hamiltonian eigenvalue problems, SQL-backed metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Hydrogen-like electronic energy levels.
2. Photon energy, frequency, and wavelength relationships.
3. Subshell orbital counts and maximum electron capacities.
4. Introductory electron-configuration tables.
5. Effective nuclear charge approximations.
6. Particle-in-a-box quantization.
7. Small Hamiltonian matrix eigenvalue calculations.
8. SQL-backed electronic-structure metadata.
9. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.
10. Provenance and workflow documentation.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not laboratory protocols, validated quantum-chemistry packages, molecular design systems, safety systems, regulatory tools, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
