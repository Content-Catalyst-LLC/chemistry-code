# Quantum Chemistry and Electronic Structure

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Quantum Chemistry and Electronic Structure

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for quantum chemistry and electronic structure, including two-level molecular-orbital mixing, electron-density grids, Hückel-model examples, basis-set convergence tables, spin-state comparisons, excited-state populations, transition-state-theory estimates, SQL-backed electronic-structure metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Two-level molecular orbital mixing.
2. Eigenvalue and eigenvector interpretation.
3. Electron-density grid scaffolds.
4. Hückel-model energy levels.
5. Basis-set convergence tables.
6. Spin-state energy comparisons.
7. Boltzmann electronic-state populations.
8. Transition-state-theory rate estimates.
9. SQL-backed electronic-structure metadata.
10. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not validated electronic-structure engines, quantum chemistry production calculations, drug-discovery tools, safety models, regulatory systems, or substitutes for professional computational chemistry practice.
