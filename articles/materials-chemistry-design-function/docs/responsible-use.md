# Responsible Use Notes

Materials chemistry evidence can influence infrastructure, energy systems, electronics, medical devices, consumer products, environmental technologies, and safety-critical designs. The examples in this folder are not validated for those purposes.

## Do not use this folder for

- engineering certification;
- clinical or medical-device decisions;
- safety-critical materials selection;
- regulatory reporting;
- infrastructure design;
- environmental marketing claims;
- procurement decisions involving health, safety, or sustainability claims.

## Recommended real-world extensions

- preserve raw characterization data and processing history;
- include replicate measurements and uncertainty;
- validate materials under realistic service conditions;
- include degradation, aging, corrosion, fatigue, fouling, and failure analysis;
- evaluate toxicity, scarcity, circularity, recyclability, and environmental fate;
- compare candidate materials against reference materials and application benchmarks;
- document assumptions behind property weighting and objective functions.
