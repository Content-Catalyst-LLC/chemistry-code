# Stoichiometry and the Quantitative Language of Reactions

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Stoichiometry and the Quantitative Language of Reactions

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for stoichiometric reasoning, including balanced reaction ratios, limiting reagent calculations, theoretical yield, percent yield, solution preparation, dilution, titration equivalence, gas stoichiometry, empirical formula inference, combustion analysis, reaction extent, process balances, SQL-backed metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Stoichiometric ratios from balanced equations.
2. Limiting reagent and excess reagent analysis.
3. Theoretical yield, actual yield, and percent yield.
4. Solution concentration and dilution.
5. Titration equivalence with coefficients.
6. Gas stoichiometry using the ideal gas equation.
7. Empirical formula inference from percent composition.
8. Combustion-analysis scaffolding.
9. Reaction extent and material-balance representations.
10. SQL-backed stoichiometry metadata.
11. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not laboratory protocols, chemical safety systems, regulatory tools, analytical validation systems, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
