# Environmental Chemistry and the Chemical Conditions of Habitability

This article folder supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

**Environmental Chemistry and the Chemical Conditions of Habitability**

The repository materials provide reproducible, educational workflows for environmental concentration screening, simple fate modeling, chemical persistence, benchmark exceedance, acid-base speciation, and provenance-aware environmental monitoring data structures.

## Contents

- `data/` — synthetic environmental monitoring data for teaching and reproducible examples
- `python/` — first-order fate modeling and benchmark exceedance workflow
- `r/` — monitoring summaries and exceedance screening
- `julia/` — environmental partitioning and river-pulse simulation
- `fortran/` — first-order decay model
- `rust/` — command-line hazard quotient and persistence example
- `go/` — monitoring hazard quotient workflow
- `c/` — mass-balance and decay example
- `cpp/` — acid-base speciation fraction model
- `sql/` — schema and sample queries for environmental monitoring records
- `docs/` — quality assurance, model assumptions, and responsible-use notes
- `notebooks/` — notebook scaffold for reproducible article analysis
- `outputs/` — generated tables, reports, figures, and provenance manifests

## Scientific scope

These examples are simplified and educational. They are designed to show how environmental chemistry connects:

- concentration measurement
- environmental media
- chemical benchmarks
- first-order persistence
- source-pathway-receptor logic
- acid-base speciation
- monitoring metadata
- threshold screening
- reproducible scientific computing

They are not regulatory tools and should not be used for compliance decisions, site remediation design, clinical exposure assessment, legal determinations, or public-health advisories without qualified expert review.
