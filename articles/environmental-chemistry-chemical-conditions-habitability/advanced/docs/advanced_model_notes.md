# Advanced Model Notes: Environmental Chemistry and the Chemical Conditions of Habitability

This advanced layer is designed to make the GitHub repository more credible as a computational science companion to the article.

## What this layer adds

- benchmark exceedance screening
- concentration-to-load conversion where flow is available
- Koc/Kd organic-carbon partitioning
- retardation-factor proxy
- mobility-factor proxy
- Henry-law air-water tendency proxy
- first-order decay constants
- persistence factor
- pH stress index
- redox stress index
- dissolved oxygen stress index
- nutrient pressure index
- ionic pressure index
- contaminant pressure index
- chemical habitability pressure index
- evidence-weighted habitability pressure
- Monte Carlo exceedance probability
- decay scenario modeling
- leaching/retardation scenario modeling
- multimedia partitioning scenario modeling
- exposure-pathway sensitivity modeling
- SQL-ready provenance structure
- lightweight tests

## Important simplifications

The workflow is intentionally educational. It does not include:

- full fugacity modeling
- calibrated multimedia fate and transport modeling
- groundwater flow modeling
- advection-dispersion transport equations
- sorption isotherm fitting
- pH-dependent speciation models
- metal complexation chemistry
- mineral saturation indices
- dissolved organic matter binding models
- bioavailability or bioaccessibility modeling
- ecological risk assessment
- human health risk assessment
- toxicokinetic or toxicodynamic modeling
- field-validated exposure factors
- uncertainty propagation based on laboratory-specific QA/QC
- regulatory compliance rules
- jurisdiction-specific thresholds
- environmental forensic source apportionment
- remediation design calculations

## Interpretation

The output indices are screening and teaching indicators. They help explain how environmental chemistry connects molecular measurements, environmental compartments, contaminant fate, exposure pathways, thresholds, and habitability, but they are not scientific findings, compliance determinations, health advisories, or site decisions.

## Responsible-use boundary

Do not use these outputs for regulatory compliance, public-health advisories, environmental forensics, remediation design, legal evidence, ecological risk assessment, drinking-water determinations, hazardous-waste determinations, property decisions, or operational monitoring.
