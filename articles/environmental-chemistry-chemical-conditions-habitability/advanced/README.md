# Advanced Computational Layer: Environmental Chemistry and the Chemical Conditions of Habitability

This folder extends the article **Environmental Chemistry and the Chemical Conditions of Habitability** with a stronger computational layer for environmental chemists, environmental scientists, monitoring teams, sustainability researchers, and risk analysts.

## Scope

This advanced layer models simplified environmental chemistry and habitability evidence using synthetic data. It includes contaminant benchmark screening, multimedia partitioning proxies, Koc/Kd partitioning, retardation factors, Henry-law air-water tendency, first-order decay, persistence, nutrient pressure, redox and pH stress, contaminant exposure pressure, receptor sensitivity, evidence-weighted habitability pressure, Monte Carlo exceedance probability, decay scenarios, leaching scenarios, multimedia partition scenarios, and SQL-ready provenance structures.

## Included files

- `data/environmental_chemistry_advanced_synthetic.csv` — synthetic environmental chemistry monitoring data
- `python/environmental_chemistry_advanced.py` — reusable advanced environmental chemistry workflow
- `python/test_environmental_chemistry_advanced.py` — lightweight reproducibility tests
- `sql/advanced_schema.sql` — SQL provenance schema
- `sql/advanced_queries.sql` — analytical query examples
- `docs/advanced_model_notes.md` — modeling assumptions and limitations
- `docs/future_research_extensions.md` — roadmap for research-grade upgrades
- `outputs/` — generated indicators, reports, scenario outputs, and manifests

## Responsible use

This code is educational and synthetic. It is not a regulatory compliance tool, public-health advisory, environmental forensic report, site-remediation design, legal analysis, ecological risk assessment, drinking-water determination, hazardous-waste determination, or operational monitoring system.
