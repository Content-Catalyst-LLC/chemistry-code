# Advanced Model Notes: Toxicology, Exposure, and Chemical Risk

This advanced layer is designed to make the GitHub repository more credible as a computational science companion to the article.

## What this layer adds

- route-specific chronic daily intake
- absorbed dose
- hazard quotient
- target-system hazard index
- mixture-group hazard index
- vulnerability-adjusted hazard
- margin of exposure
- cancer-risk proxy
- evidence-weighted risk index
- Monte Carlo uncertainty intervals
- probability that hazard quotient exceeds 1
- exposure-reduction scenario modeling
- body-weight and vulnerability scenario modeling
- mixture contribution analysis
- SQL-ready provenance structure
- lightweight tests

## Important simplifications

The workflow is intentionally educational. It does not include:

- regulatory-grade exposure assessment
- route-specific agency guidance implementation
- benchmark dose modeling
- PBPK or PBTK modeling
- toxicodynamic modeling
- validated cancer-slope-factor interpretation
- chemical-specific bioavailability models
- dermal permeability modeling
- inhalation dosimetry adjustment
- life-stage-specific exposure factors
- epidemiological causal inference
- clinical diagnosis
- occupational exposure compliance
- environmental site cleanup determination
- cumulative risk assessment with nonchemical stressors
- environmental justice screening tools
- laboratory-specific QA/QC uncertainty models
- jurisdiction-specific regulatory thresholds

## Interpretation

The output indices are screening and teaching indicators. They help explain how toxicology connects hazard, exposure, dose, vulnerability, mixtures, uncertainty, and risk characterization, but they are not scientific findings, compliance determinations, health advisories, diagnoses, causation conclusions, or cleanup decisions.

## Responsible-use boundary

Do not use these outputs for public-health advice, clinical diagnosis, legal claims, regulatory compliance, workplace safety determinations, product safety claims, site remediation decisions, drinking-water determinations, or communication about real community risk without qualified professional review.
