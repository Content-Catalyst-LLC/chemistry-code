# Intermolecular Forces and the Chemistry of Condensed Matter

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Intermolecular Forces and the Chemistry of Condensed Matter

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for intermolecular forces and condensed matter, including Lennard-Jones potentials, simple Coulombic interactions, vapor-pressure fitting, surface-tension summaries, radial-distribution scaffolds, phase-property tables, condensed-phase descriptors, SQL-backed metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Lennard-Jones potential energy curves.
2. Simplified Coulombic interaction calculations.
3. Pair-interaction parameter tables.
4. Vapor-pressure fitting using a Clausius-Clapeyron-style approximation.
5. Surface-tension and condensed-phase property summaries.
6. Radial-distribution histogram scaffolds.
7. Phase-property classification.
8. SQL-backed condensed-matter chemistry records.
9. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.
10. Provenance and workflow documentation.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not production molecular simulations, validated force fields, laboratory safety systems, regulatory tools, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
