# Advanced Model Notes: Water Chemistry and Environmental Monitoring

This advanced layer is designed to make the GitHub repository more credible as a computational science companion to the article.

## What this layer adds

- benchmark ratio screening
- concentration-to-load conversion
- dissolved oxygen deficit
- dissolved oxygen stress index
- pH pressure
- conductivity and chloride pressure
- turbidity pressure
- nutrient enrichment index
- metal/metalloid pressure index
- composite water-quality pressure index
- QA-weighted evidence score
- storm-pulse first-order decay scenario
- nutrient load scenario grid
- dissolved oxygen sag and recovery scenario
- SQL-ready provenance structure
- lightweight tests

## Important simplifications

The workflow is intentionally educational. It does not include:

- jurisdiction-specific regulatory criteria
- hardness-adjusted aquatic-life metal criteria
- censored-data statistics
- storm hydrograph modeling
- watershed routing
- calibrated dissolved oxygen models
- sensor drift correction
- uncertainty propagation
- laboratory method comparability
- drinking-water compliance rules
- aquatic toxicity modeling

## Interpretation

The output indices are screening and teaching indicators. They help explain how water chemistry connects measurements to monitoring evidence, but they are not scientific findings, compliance determinations, or health advisories.

## Responsible-use boundary

Do not use these outputs for drinking-water safety decisions, public-health advisories, regulatory compliance, discharge permitting, legal evidence, site remediation, fisheries management, or operational monitoring.
