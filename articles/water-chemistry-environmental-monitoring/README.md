# Water Chemistry and Environmental Monitoring

This article folder supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

**Water Chemistry and Environmental Monitoring**

The repository materials provide reproducible, educational workflows for water-quality screening, nutrient load estimation, pH and dissolved oxygen interpretation, hardness and alkalinity calculations, benchmark exceedance summaries, and provenance-aware water monitoring data structures.

## Contents

- `data/` — synthetic water-quality monitoring data for surface water, groundwater, lakes, and stormwater
- `python/` — water-quality screening, nutrient loads, pH flags, and reporting
- `r/` — monitoring summaries and exceedance counts
- `julia/` — nutrient load and simple oxygen deficit calculations
- `fortran/` — dissolved oxygen saturation approximation
- `rust/` — command-line water-quality screening example
- `go/` — nutrient load and benchmark screening example
- `c/` — pH and conductivity screening example
- `cpp/` — alkalinity and hardness calculation example
- `sql/` — schema and sample queries for water monitoring records
- `docs/` — QA/QC notes, model assumptions, and responsible-use guidance
- `notebooks/` — reproducible notebook scaffold
- `outputs/` — generated tables, reports, figures, and provenance manifests

## Scientific scope

These examples are simplified and educational. They are designed to show how water chemistry connects:

- concentration and load
- pH and alkalinity
- hardness and dissolved ions
- dissolved oxygen and aquatic-life conditions
- nutrients and eutrophication screening
- trace metals and benchmark comparisons
- stormwater, groundwater, surface-water, and lake monitoring
- field/laboratory quality assurance
- structured environmental data provenance

They are not regulatory tools and should not be used for compliance decisions, drinking-water safety determinations, remediation design, legal determinations, public-health advisories, or emergency response without qualified expert review.
