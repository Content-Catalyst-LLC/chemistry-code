# QA/QC Notes for Water Chemistry Data

A credible water-monitoring workflow should preserve enough information to make data auditable.

## Recommended fields

- sample identifier
- site identifier
- date and time
- latitude and longitude
- medium and water-body type
- sampling depth
- field parameters
- flow or stage condition
- analyte
- concentration
- unit
- detection limit
- reporting limit
- analytical method
- filtration status
- preservation method
- holding time
- blank results
- duplicate results
- matrix spike recovery
- calibration checks
- laboratory identifier
- qualifier flags
- benchmark source and context
- data-review status

## Common quality issues

- nitrate reported as nitrate versus nitrate as nitrogen
- phosphate reported as phosphate versus phosphorus
- total versus dissolved metals confused
- filtered versus unfiltered samples merged
- detection limits omitted
- pH measured after holding rather than in the field
- storm-event samples compared directly to baseflow samples
- grab samples treated as annual averages
- microbial data interpreted without holding-time context
- units converted incorrectly
- benchmarks applied outside their intended use

## Practical principle

Water-quality data should allow another qualified reviewer to reconstruct how the sample was collected, analyzed, qualified, compared, and interpreted.
