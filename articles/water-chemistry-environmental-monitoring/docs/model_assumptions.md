# Model Assumptions

The computational examples in this folder are simplified teaching tools.

## Benchmark screening

The ratio

R = measured concentration / benchmark

indicates whether a value exceeds a selected comparison value. It does not determine legal compliance, human-health risk, aquatic-life impairment, source attribution, or remediation responsibility.

## Nutrient load

The nutrient-load examples use:

kg/day = mg/L * L/s * 0.0864

This assumes concentration and flow are representative of the same time interval. Real load estimation may require continuous flow, concentration-discharge relationships, storm-event sampling, censored-data methods, and uncertainty modeling.

## pH screening

The pH screening range of 6.5 to 9.0 is used for educational illustration. Real criteria depend on jurisdiction, designated use, organism sensitivity, site conditions, and applicable standards.

## Dissolved oxygen

The dissolved oxygen examples are simplified. Real oxygen dynamics depend on temperature, salinity, pressure, reaeration, respiration, photosynthesis, stratification, sediment oxygen demand, organic loading, flow, and time of day.
