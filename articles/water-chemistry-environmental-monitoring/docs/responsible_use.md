# Responsible Use Notes

These files are educational examples for chemistry communication, computational learning, and reproducible workflow design.

They should not be used as:

- drinking-water safety determinations
- regulatory compliance tools
- emergency-response guidance
- public-health advisories
- legal evidence
- site-remediation designs
- watershed impairment determinations
- wastewater permit evaluations
- clinical or toxicological diagnoses

Water-quality decisions can affect public health, ecosystems, infrastructure, property, and public trust. Real applications require validated methods, qualified experts, legally appropriate standards, uncertainty analysis, chain-of-custody procedures where necessary, and transparent communication with affected communities.

The examples intentionally use synthetic data.
