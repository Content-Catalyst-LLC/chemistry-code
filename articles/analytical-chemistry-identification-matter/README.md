# Analytical Chemistry and the Identification of Matter

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Analytical Chemistry and the Identification of Matter

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for analytical chemistry, including calibration curves, blank statistics, LOD and LOQ calculations, unknown concentration estimates, spike recovery, precision metrics, chromatographic resolution, Beer-Lambert quantification, spectral matching, quality-control flags, SQL-backed analytical metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Linear calibration.
2. Unknown concentration estimation.
3. Blank statistics.
4. Limit of detection and limit of quantification.
5. Replicate precision and relative standard deviation.
6. Spike recovery.
7. Chromatographic resolution.
8. Beer-Lambert quantification.
9. Simple spectral similarity scoring.
10. Analytical quality-control flags.
11. SQL-backed analytical-method metadata.
12. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not validated laboratory information management systems, regulatory systems, clinical tools, forensic tools, safety systems, or substitutes for professional analytical chemistry practice.
