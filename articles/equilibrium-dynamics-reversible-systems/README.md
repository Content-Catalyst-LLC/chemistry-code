# Equilibrium and the Dynamics of Reversible Systems

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Equilibrium and the Dynamics of Reversible Systems

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for chemical equilibrium, reversible reaction dynamics, reaction quotients, equilibrium constants, Gibbs free energy, Le Chatelier perturbations, van 't Hoff analysis, solubility products, simple activity corrections, coupled equilibria, dynamic approach to equilibrium, SQL-backed metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Reaction quotient and equilibrium-direction calculations.
2. Free-energy changes from Q/K relationships.
3. Algebraic equilibrium solving for simple reversible reactions.
4. Dynamic reversible ODE approach to equilibrium.
5. Le Chatelier perturbation examples.
6. van 't Hoff temperature-dependence fitting.
7. Gas equilibrium relationships.
8. Solubility product and ion-product comparisons.
9. Simple activity coefficient scaffolding.
10. SQL-backed equilibrium metadata.
11. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not validated thermodynamic databases, laboratory protocols, environmental compliance tools, industrial process models, pharmaceutical speciation tools, or substitutes for professional chemical practice.
