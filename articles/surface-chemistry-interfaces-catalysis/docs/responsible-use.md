# Responsible Use Notes

Surface chemistry and catalysis evidence can influence energy systems, emissions control, chemical manufacturing, environmental technologies, sensors, coatings, batteries, and process safety. The examples in this folder are not validated for those purposes.

## Do not use this folder for

- catalyst scale-up;
- process engineering;
- safety-critical reaction design;
- environmental performance claims;
- regulatory reporting;
- industrial catalyst selection;
- hazardous reaction control.

## Recommended real-world extensions

- preserve catalyst synthesis, pretreatment, activation, and reaction history;
- include raw characterization data and uncertainty;
- report surface area, site density, particle size, dispersion, support, and porosity;
- check heat and mass-transfer limitations;
- measure product balance, selectivity, and byproducts;
- track deactivation, poisoning, regeneration, and time-on-stream stability;
- consider critical materials, toxicity, energy demand, waste, and end-of-life recovery;
- validate mechanisms with in situ or operando evidence where possible.
