# Responsible Use Notes

Mass spectrometry is widely used in pharmaceutical, clinical, environmental, food-safety, forensic, anti-doping, industrial, and regulatory contexts. The examples in this folder are not validated for those purposes.

## Do not use this folder for

- clinical diagnosis or treatment;
- forensic identification;
- regulatory compliance;
- environmental enforcement;
- pharmaceutical release;
- industrial quality certification;
- hazardous-material identification;
- safety-critical chemical decisions.

## Recommended real-world extensions

- preserve raw vendor files and open-format conversions such as mzML where appropriate;
- document ionization mode, source conditions, analyzer, mass calibration, and acquisition method;
- use blanks, standards, internal standards, isotope-labeled standards, and quality controls;
- report mass tolerance, isotope scoring, retention windows, fragment evidence, and confidence levels;
- confirm important identifications using reference standards and orthogonal evidence;
- distinguish detected features from confirmed compounds.
