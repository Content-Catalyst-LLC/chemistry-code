# Responsible Use Notes

Nanomaterial evidence can influence medicine, environmental monitoring, consumer products, coatings, sensors, electronics, food-contact materials, water systems, and occupational exposure. The examples in this folder are not validated for those purposes.

## Do not use this folder for

- clinical or nanomedicine decisions;
- toxicological assessment;
- environmental release assessment;
- regulatory reporting;
- product safety claims;
- occupational exposure claims;
- food-contact or medical-device qualification;
- environmental marketing claims.

## Recommended real-world extensions

- measure nanomaterials in relevant media, not only pure solvent;
- report primary particle size, hydrodynamic size, aggregation, and surface chemistry;
- distinguish mass concentration, particle number concentration, and surface-area dose;
- include replicate measurements, uncertainty, and method limitations;
- evaluate dissolution, transformation, corona formation, and degradation;
- preserve synthesis, purification, storage, and handling records;
- assess worker exposure and end-of-life pathways where relevant;
- use reference materials and validated protocols for consequential decisions.
