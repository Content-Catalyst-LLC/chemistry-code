# Chemical Biology and Molecular Intervention in Living Systems

This article repository supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

Chemical Biology and Molecular Intervention in Living Systems

The repository provides reproducible computational scaffolding for chemical biology, including dose-response modeling, target-engagement examples, probe quality scoring, selectivity tables, chemoproteomic competition scaffolds, perturbation-vector analysis, network-response summaries, SQL-backed chemical-biology metadata, notebook scaffolding, and cross-language examples in Python, R, Julia, Fortran, Rust, Go, C, and C++.

No university affiliation is implied by this repository. The design goal is advanced reproducible computational rigor: clear assumptions, transparent calculations, synthetic educational data, provenance records, responsible-use notes, and reusable scientific workflow structure.

## Scientific Focus

The workflows demonstrate:

1. Dose-response modeling.
2. Fractional target occupancy.
3. Target engagement calculation.
4. Probe quality scoring.
5. Selectivity-ratio calculation.
6. Chemoproteomic competition scaffolds.
7. Perturbation-vector analysis.
8. Chemical-genetic phenotype summaries.
9. Network-response descriptors.
10. SQL-backed chemical-biology metadata.
11. Cross-language numerical reproducibility.

These examples are educational and methodological scaffolds. They are not validated drug-discovery tools, clinical tools, biological safety systems, regulatory systems, therapeutic design systems, or substitutes for professional chemical biology practice.
