# Geochemistry and the Chemical History of Earth

This article folder supports the Chemistry knowledge-series article:

**Geochemistry and the Chemical History of Earth**

The repository materials provide reproducible, educational workflows for major-oxide geochemistry, simplified Chemical Index of Alteration calculation, isotope delta notation, parent-daughter radiometric age estimation, trace-element ratios, rock-type summaries, and provenance-aware geochemical data structures.

## Contents

- `data/` — synthetic rock, sediment, and weathering geochemistry data
- `python/` — weathering index, isotope notation, radiometric age, and trace-element screening
- `r/` — rock-type summary and simplified geochemical indicators
- `julia/` — CIA, isotope delta, and two-component mixing examples
- `fortran/` — radiometric decay and age calculation
- `rust/` — command-line geochemical index example
- `go/` — geochemical ratio and age calculation example
- `c/` — isotope delta notation example
- `cpp/` — simplified rare-earth-element normalization example
- `sql/` — schema and sample queries for geochemical sample records
- `docs/` — QA/QC notes, model assumptions, and responsible-use guidance
- `notebooks/` — reproducible notebook scaffold
- `outputs/` — generated tables, reports, figures, and provenance manifests

## Scientific scope

These examples are simplified and educational. They are designed to show how geochemistry connects:

- rock chemistry
- mineral and oxide composition
- weathering indices
- trace-element ratios
- isotope notation
- radiometric dating
- crustal recycling
- redox history
- Earth-system chemical archives
- data provenance and analytical QA/QC

They are not professional geochemical interpretations, mining assessments, geochronology reports, regulatory determinations, contamination assessments, legal evidence, or resource estimates.
