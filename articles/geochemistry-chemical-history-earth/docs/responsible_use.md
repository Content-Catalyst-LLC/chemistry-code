# Responsible Use Notes

These files are educational examples for chemistry communication, computational learning, and reproducible workflow design.

They should not be used as:

- professional geochronology reports
- mining or resource assessments
- exploration decisions
- contamination assessments
- legal evidence
- regulatory submissions
- public-health advisories
- planetary protection assessments
- investment guidance

Geochemical interpretation can affect land use, mining, remediation, heritage sites, environmental risk, climate interpretation, and public trust. Real applications require qualified geoscientists, validated methods, field context, uncertainty analysis, appropriate standards, and transparent communication.

The examples intentionally use synthetic data.
