# Model Assumptions

The computational examples in this folder are simplified teaching tools.

## Chemical Index of Alteration

The example uses:

CIA = 100 * Al2O3 / (Al2O3 + CaO + Na2O + K2O)

The teaching workflow uses oxide weight percentages and does not correct CaO*. Research workflows should convert oxides to molar proportions, correct calcium for non-silicate phases, examine mineralogy, and account for sediment sorting, potassium metasomatism, parent-rock composition, and diagenesis.

## Radiometric age

The simplified age expression is:

t = (1/lambda) ln(1 + D/P)

It assumes no initial daughter, closed-system behavior, no inheritance, no daughter loss, no metamorphic resetting, and no analytical uncertainty. Professional geochronology requires isotope-specific methods, standards, corrections, concordance testing, mineral context, and uncertainty propagation.

## Isotope delta notation

The isotope delta examples demonstrate notation only. Real isotope analysis requires reference standards, instrument correction, normalization, blank correction, uncertainty, and laboratory QA/QC.

## Trace-element ratios

Trace-element ratios can be useful indicators, but they are not self-interpreting. They require petrologic, mineralogical, stratigraphic, and analytical context.
